Understanding Felonies vs. Misdemeanors in California

California classifies crimes as either felonies, misdemeanors, or infractions. Understanding the difference is crucial:

Felonies

The most serious crimes, punishable by state prison time (16 months or more), significant fines, and long-term consequences including loss of voting rights and firearm ownership.

Misdemeanors

Less serious offenses punishable by up to 1 year in county jail and/or fines up to $1,000. Still creates a criminal record that can affect employment and housing.

Many crimes in California are "wobblers" that can be charged as either a felony or misdemeanor depending on the circumstances. An experienced defense attorney can often negotiate to reduce a felony to a misdemeanor.

The Criminal Court Process in Sutter & Yuba County

Understanding what to expect can help reduce anxiety about your case:

1. Arrest & Booking

After arrest, you'll be booked at the county jail. You may be released on bail, OR (own recognizance), or held until arraignment.

2. Arraignment

Your first court appearance where charges are formally read and you enter a plea. This must occur within 48 hours of arrest (excluding weekends/holidays).

3. Preliminary Hearing (Felonies)

For felony charges, the prosecution must show probable cause at a preliminary hearing before the case can proceed to trial.

4. Pre-Trial Motions

Your attorney can file motions to suppress evidence, dismiss charges, or challenge the prosecution's case before trial.

5. Plea Negotiations

Many cases are resolved through plea bargaining. An experienced attorney can often negotiate reduced charges or lighter sentences.

6. Trial

If no plea agreement is reached, your case goes to trial where the prosecution must prove guilt beyond a reasonable doubt.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if I'm arrested?

Exercise your right to remain silent and ask for an attorney immediately. Do not answer questions or make statements without legal representation. Be polite but firm. Contact Tony Carlos Law at (530) 645-2551 as soon as possible.

Can I get my charges reduced or dismissed?

Potentially, yes. Depending on the evidence, circumstances, and your criminal history, charges can sometimes be reduced to lesser offenses or dismissed entirely. Every case is different - contact us to discuss your specific situation.

Will I go to jail?

Not necessarily. Many criminal cases are resolved without jail time through probation, diversion programs, community service, or other alternatives. The outcome depends on the charges, your history, and the strength of your defense.
