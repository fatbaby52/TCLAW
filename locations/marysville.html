Yuba County Superior Court

Criminal cases for Marysville and Yuba County residents are heard at the Yuba County Superior Court, a historic courthouse located at 215 5th Street in downtown Marysville. Understanding the local court system is essential for effective legal representation:

  • Felony Cases: Typically heard in Department 3
  • Misdemeanor Cases: Usually assigned to Department 2
  • Arraignments: Occur within 48 hours of arrest (excluding weekends/holidays)
  • Drug Court Program: Alternative sentencing available for qualifying cases

Frequently Asked Questions - Marysville Criminal Defense

Where is the Yuba County Superior Court located?

The Yuba County Superior Court is located at 215 5th Street in downtown Marysville, CA 95901. This historic courthouse handles all criminal, civil, and family law matters for Yuba County residents. Felony cases are typically heard in Department 3, while misdemeanor cases are usually assigned to Department 2.

What happens if I'm arrested in Marysville?

If you're arrested in Marysville, you'll typically be booked at the Yuba County Jail. You have the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney. Exercise these rights. Don't answer questions without your lawyer present. Call Tony Carlos Law at (530) 645-2551 immediately to discuss your case.

How are Marysville DUI cases handled?

Marysville DUI cases are prosecuted by the Yuba County District Attorney and heard in Yuba County Superior Court. You'll face both criminal charges and a separate DMV hearing to determine license suspension. It's crucial to request a DMV hearing within 10 days of your arrest. Contact us immediately after a DUI arrest.

Does Yuba County have a drug court program?

Yes, Yuba County offers a Drug Court program for qualifying defendants. This alternative sentencing program focuses on treatment and rehabilitation rather than incarceration. Tony Carlos can evaluate whether your case qualifies for Drug Court and advocate for your participation.

Can military members from Beale AFB hire a civilian attorney?

Yes, service members facing civilian criminal charges in Yuba County can and should hire a civilian criminal defense attorney. Military members have the same rights as civilians in state court. Tony Carlos has experience defending Beale AFB personnel in Yuba County courts.
